Most of the work that makes something feel good is invisible. Nobody thanks you
for the spacing between two lines, or the way a link fades instead of blinking.
But they feel it. They stay a little longer. They trust the thing a little more.

That's the whole bet of minimalism: if you remove everything that doesn't matter,
the few things left had better be **excellent**.

## Subtract until it hurts, then stop

It's easy to add. A new section, another button, one more idea on the page. Hard
is taking things away until only the essential remains — and then making that
essential thing quietly perfect.

- One typeface, used with conviction.
- One accent color, earned.
- One idea per page, finished.

> Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is
> nothing left to take away.

I'd rather publish one page I've sweated over than ten I haven't. This is that
