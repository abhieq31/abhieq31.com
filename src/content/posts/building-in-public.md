---
title: Building in Public
subtitle: Sharing the work before it's finished
date: 2025-12-05
---

There's a quiet kind of leverage in showing your work early.

When you build in the open, three things happen. People who care find you sooner.
You get feedback while it's still cheap to change course. And you build a record
of how you think, which turns out to be more interesting than any single result.

The fear is that someone will copy you or judge the rough edges. In practice,
almost no one copies — execution is the hard part — and the rough edges are what
make it relatable.

Post the draft. Ship the half-built thing. Narrate the process. The audience for
your finished work is built while the work is still unfinished.
