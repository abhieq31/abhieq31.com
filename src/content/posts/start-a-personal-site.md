Social platforms are great for distribution and terrible for permanence. Posts
sink, accounts get locked, and the design changes under your feet.

A personal site flips that. You own the domain, the words, and the layout. Years
from now it still loads, still reads the way you wrote it, and still points to
you instead of a platform.

It doesn't need to be fancy. A clean page, a list of posts, and a way for people
to subscribe is enough. Start small, publish often, and let it compound.

The best time to start was years ago. The second best time is today.
